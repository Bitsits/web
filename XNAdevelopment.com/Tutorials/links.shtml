  • XNA Creator's Club - Microsoft's official forums for questions and discussions about XNA Development. Provides support, tutorials, samples and more for your XNA Game development needs.
  • XNA Developer Center - Microsoft's site for XNA development, news and support.
  • XNA Team Blog - the official blog for the XNA Development team at Microsoft.
  • Let's Kill Dave - David Weller's blog. David works for Microsoft as a Game Developer Community Manager. Very active in the game community and author of a couple of game development books. He currently has an XNA book in the works, looking forward to seeing that come out.
  • The ZBuffer - the number one site for news and information relating to XNA. Tutorials, links, news all XNA all the time.
  • Ziggyware - one of the best places to get your XNA news. Ziggy is constantly staying on top of what is going on in the community and getting that information out to everyone. Definitely a "must bookmark" site for XNA.
  • XNA Wiki - XNA Wiki is a place for XNA Game Studio users to collaborate and share code, techniques, and tutorials to aid others in the pursuit of game development for all.
  • Mykre's Blog - Mykre has been very active in the gaming community and the MSDN forums for quite a while. Very well respected, he and the ZBuffer have been two of my best resources for game development.
  • Machaira's Space -a blog dedicated to XNA development, news and gaming in general and managed by one of the top answerers in the MSDN forums for XNA.
  • Blue Rose Games - this XNA development site (formerly XNA101.com) focuses on XNA development for game developers without previous C# experience. There are lots of tutorials, code samples and information on XNA development for developers of all skill sets. This site is also a great resource for people wanting to get started with 3D development with XNA.
  • exDream - a site run by one of the best DirectX and XNA developers outside of Microsoft. Also the author of Professional XNA Game Programming.
  • Focused Games - a great site kept by a very active community member Krisc from the Efnet IRC channel. Focused Games has been making great headway on a Desktop Tower Defense clone in XNA and has other large projects in the works.
  • Alan Phipps.com - VB and XNA Game Development - this site has XNA tutorials for those still doing Visual Basic game development. While it's my personal stance that I think you should just learn C# to use XNA, these tutorials are still a good resource for people to scared to take the plunge right now (like I was for years).
